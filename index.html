Welcome

I’m a Computer Science student at ENSEEIHT, driven by the intersection of data, strategy, and high-speed performance. My goal is to apply my skills in data analysis and AI to one of the most demanding and interesting environments I know of : competitive motorsport.

This portfolio follows my journey — from experimenting with reinforcement learning models to preparing an international mobility project that connects with my goals as an engineer.

My Engineering Courses

Project: Reinforcement Learning AI for "Snake"

  • Context: As a personal side project, I trained a simple AI agent to play and master the classic game “Snake.”
  • My Role: I started from a given model from a tutorial but focused on the real engineering challenge: configuring, training, and tuning the model rather than rewriting it from scratch.
  • Key Learnings & Reflection: I managed several training cycles, tested different parameters, and studied how each configuration affected the performance. This experience strengthened my interest not only in AI theory but also in the process of improving a system.

International Mobility

Mobility Goals: Ireland & The Netherlands would be my first choice

I aim to study in a major European tech hub to develop my skills. After reviewing the available agreements, there seems to be good opportunities for my field in Ireland and The Netherlands.

The most suitable options are through "Free Mover" or "Paying Agreement" programs to get a master in my third year in one of those universities. Moreover, I really appreciate those countries and the opportunites that I could get by studying in another country for a whole year.

Project 1: TU Delft (Netherlands)

Why: TU Delft, available as a “Free Mover” destination, is world-class in engineering, applied mathematics, and AI. I would target their Computer Science & Engineering or Applied Mathematics departments, both completly aligned with my goals.

Project 2: Dublin City University (Ireland)

Why: DCU is listed with a Paying Agreement that covers all departments. Its School of Computing is particularly strong in Data Science and AI which is an ideal match for the kind of applied and data-driven work I aim to pursue.

Project 3: University College Cork (Ireland)

Why: UCC, also a “Free Mover” option, has a highly regarded School of Computer Science and Information Technology. Their research in AI and data analytics provides another relevant option for my mobility project.
